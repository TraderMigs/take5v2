Pickleball Quick Guide

New to pickleball or just looking for a quick refresher? This guide explains the basics in plain English so you can understand the game, search for places to play, and show up with a little more confidence.

What is pickleball?

Pickleball is a paddle sport that blends parts of tennis, badminton, and ping-pong. Players hit a plastic ball over a low net on a smaller court, usually as singles or doubles.

Basic rules

Serve underhand, let the ball bounce once on each side after the serve, avoid volleying inside the kitchen, and keep the ball in bounds. Most casual games are simple once you learn the flow.

Beginner scoring

Only the serving side usually scores. Games are commonly played to 11, and you normally need to win by 2. Doubles scoring can feel weird at first, but it gets easier after a few games.

The kitchen

The kitchen is the non-volley zone near the net. You can step into it, but you cannot volley the ball while standing in it. This rule keeps the game from turning into constant net smashes.

How to find games

Search for pickleball courts, open play, clubs, leagues, lessons, or tournaments in your city. Parks, gyms, community centers, resorts, and sports clubs often host beginner-friendly sessions.

Beginner mistakes

Most beginners swing too hard, rush the net, ignore the kitchen, or forget to control the return. Keep the ball in play first. Power matters less than placement and patience.

Pickleball FAQ

Fast answers for new players searching for pickleball courts, games, and rules.

How do I find pickleball courts near me?

Use the Take5 court finder and type your city, neighborhood, or country. The search automatically focuses on pickleball courts, clubs, open play, lessons, and local games.

Can beginners play pickleball?

Yes. Pickleball is popular with beginners because the court is smaller, rallies can start slowly, and many clubs offer open play or beginner sessions.

What equipment do I need?

You need a paddle, pickleball balls, comfortable shoes, and a court. Many beginner groups or clubs may have loaner paddles available.

What is open play?

Open play means players can rotate into games without needing a full private group. It is one of the easiest ways to meet local pickleball players.

Is pickleball good for a quick break?

Yes. A short rally, drill, or mini-game can help you reset, move your body, and clear your head without needing a long workout.

Does Take5 track my location?
